Welcome & Rules

⚡ Welcome to Siivouspartio

This is your guide to doing great work. Read every page. Follow every step. Ask your team leader if you do not understand something. You are a cleaning pro — act like one!

📋 Your Job Responsibilities

These are the things you MUST do for every shift. No exceptions.

1

Apply to Open Shifts on Connecteam

Open the Connecteam app on your phone. Go to the "Open Shifts" tab. Look at the shifts that are free. Pick the ones you want to work. Tap "Apply". Wait for your team leader to say yes. Do this every day so you get enough work.

2

Read the Shift Requirements

Before you go to a job, read the shift notes carefully. The notes tell you: what kind of cleaning to do, what the customer wants, what tools to bring, and any special things to know. If you do not read the notes, you might do the wrong thing. Always read them.

3

Arrive at the Location on Time

Go to the address in the shift notes. Be there at the exact time — not late, not too early. If you will be late, tell your team leader right away. Being late makes the customer unhappy and hurts our company. Plan your trip before you leave home.

4

Clock In and Clock Out

When you arrive at the job, open Connecteam and tap "Clock In". This starts your work timer. When you finish all the cleaning, tap "Clock Out". You must do this for every single shift. If you forget, your hours will not be counted.

🧹 Home Cleaning Guide

The most common job. A full clean of the customer's home — every room, every surface.

🎯 The Goal

When you leave, the home should look and smell fresh. Every surface should be dust-free. Every floor should be clean. The customer should say "wow!"

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Walk through the whole home first

Look at every room. See what needs the most work. Check the shift notes for any special requests from the customer.

2

Start from the TOP — work DOWN

Always clean high things first (shelves, light fixtures, tops of cabinets). Dust falls down. If you clean the floor first, it will get dirty again.

3

Clean the kitchen

Wipe all counters and tables. Clean the stove top. Wipe cabinet doors. Clean the sink with degreaser. Empty the trash can and put in a new bag.

4

Clean the bathrooms

Scrub the toilet inside and outside. Clean the sink and mirror. Scrub the shower or bathtub. Wipe tiles. Mop the floor. Use disinfectant.

5

Clean the bedrooms and living areas

Dust all surfaces (tables, shelves, TV, window sills). Make the beds if the customer wants it. Pick up visible trash.

6

Vacuum all floors

Vacuum every room, including under beds and sofas if you can reach. Vacuum rugs and carpets carefully. Move slowly so the vacuum picks up everything.

7

Mop all hard floors

After vacuuming, mop all tile, wood, and stone floors with a damp mop. Do NOT use too much water — wet floors can damage wood.

8

Final check

Walk through every room one more time. Look for spots you missed. Check mirrors for streaks. Make sure trash bags are tied and by the door.

💡
Recommended sequence: Kitchen → bathrooms → bedrooms → living room → floors. Following the same order consistently ensures complete coverage.

📦 Move-Out Cleaning

Deep cleaning for empty apartments and homes. This is essential for getting the security deposit back or preparing a property for new tenants.

🎯 The Goal

Leave the space spotless and ready for the next occupant. Every surface must be pristine. This is more thorough than regular cleaning because the space is empty and every detail shows.

⏱️
Time estimate: Move-out cleaning takes 2–4x longer than regular cleaning. An empty 2-bedroom apartment typically requires 4–6 hours for thorough cleaning.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Ensure space is completely empty

Confirm all furniture, belongings, and trash are removed. You cannot clean properly with items in the way. If anything remains, ask the customer or team leader what to do.

2

Start with high dusting

Dust ceiling corners, light fixtures, ceiling fans, air vents, and tops of door frames. Use an extension duster or ladder. Cobwebs and dust are very visible in empty spaces.

3

Clean all walls and baseboards

Wipe down walls with all-purpose cleaner, paying attention to light switches, fingerprints, and scuff marks. Clean all baseboards thoroughly — these collect a lot of dust.

4

Deep clean the kitchen

Clean inside and outside of all cabinets and drawers. Scrub the oven, stove, and range hood. Clean the refrigerator inside and out (if staying). Scrub sink and faucets until they shine. Wipe all countertops and backsplashes.

5

Deep clean all bathrooms

Scrub toilet, sink, shower/tub, and all tiles. Clean mirrors spotless. Scrub grout lines. Clean inside cabinets and medicine cabinets. Polish all chrome fixtures.

6

Clean all windows and window sills

Clean windows inside and out if accessible. Clean window tracks and sills — these often have dirt buildup. Wipe window frames.

7

Clean all doors and door frames

Wipe both sides of every door. Clean door handles and frames. Check for scuff marks and fingerprints.

8

Clean inside all closets

Vacuum or sweep closet floors. Wipe shelving and hanging rods. Clean the closet door and frame.

9

Vacuum or sweep all floors

Vacuum carpets thoroughly, including edges and corners. Sweep all hard floors. Move into corners and under where furniture was.

10

Mop all hard floors

Mop all tile, vinyl, and hard floors. Change water frequently. Empty spaces show every streak and spot, so mop carefully.

11

Final walkthrough

Walk through the entire space. Check every room, cabinet, closet. Look for anything missed. Test that everything is clean and dry.

📋
Checklist reminder: Empty spaces reveal every detail. Pay special attention to corners, edges, baseboards, and areas that were hidden by furniture. Landlords and new tenants will inspect thoroughly.

🚿 Bathroom Deep Clean

A very detailed cleaning of the bathroom. This goes deeper than a normal home clean.

1

Spray everything first

Spray the toilet, sink, shower, tub, and tiles with bathroom cleaner. Let it sit for 5–10 minutes. The chemicals need time to break down the dirt.

2

Scrub the shower/tub

Use a scrub brush on all tiles and grout lines. Pay extra attention to corners where mold likes to grow. Scrub the shower head and faucet handles.

3

Deep clean the toilet

Put toilet cleaner inside the bowl. Scrub with a toilet brush. Wipe the outside, the seat, the lid, and behind the toilet. Use disinfectant spray.

4

Clean the sink and mirror

Scrub the sink basin and faucet. Remove limescale with lime remover. Clean the mirror with glass cleaner — use a microfiber cloth to avoid streaks.

5

Scrub tile walls and floor

Scrub wall tiles near the shower. Scrub the floor on your hands and knees if needed. Get into every corner and behind the toilet.

6

Rinse everything

Rinse all surfaces with clean water. Make sure no chemical residue is left. Dry the mirror and faucets with a clean cloth for a shiny finish.

⚠️
Safety: Never mix bleach with other chemicals. It can make dangerous gas. Always open a window or turn on the fan for fresh air when using strong cleaners.

🪟 Window Cleaning

Crystal clear windows make a huge difference. This guide shows you how to get streak-free glass every time.

1

Remove dust and loose dirt

Use a dry cloth or brush to wipe the window frame and sill first. Remove cobwebs from corners. This stops mud from forming when you spray.

2

Spray window cleaner on the glass

Spray the whole glass surface evenly. Or dip a window washer (the fuzzy tool) in a bucket of soapy water and scrub the glass.

3

Squeegee from top to bottom

Start at the top left corner. Pull the squeegee straight across. Wipe the squeegee blade with a cloth after each pass. Overlap each stroke a little so you don't leave lines.

4

Wipe the edges

Use a dry microfiber cloth to wipe the edges and corners where the squeegee can't reach.

5

Clean the window sill and frame

Wipe the frame with a damp cloth. Clean the sill. If there is mold, use mold remover. Dry everything.

6

Do both sides

Clean inside AND outside if the customer asked for it. Be very careful with high windows. Use an extension pole — never stand on unsafe things.

💡
Best conditions: Do NOT clean windows in direct sunlight. The cleaner dries too fast and leaves streaks. Cloudy days or shade = best results.

🔥 Oven Cleaning

Ovens get very dirty with burnt food and grease. This is how you make them look new again.

1

Make sure the oven is OFF and cool

Never clean a hot oven. Turn it off and wait until it is cool enough to touch.

2

Remove the racks and trays

Take out all oven racks, trays, and loose parts. Put them in the sink or bathtub with hot soapy water to soak.

3

Spray oven cleaner inside

Spray a thick layer of oven cleaner on all inside surfaces — walls, floor, ceiling, and door glass. Avoid the heating elements. Let it sit for 15–20 minutes (or as the product says).

4

Scrub the inside

Use a scrub sponge or brush to scrub off the softened grease and burnt food. Scrub hard in the corners and bottom where food collects.

5

Wipe clean with a damp cloth

Wipe all surfaces with a damp cloth until no cleaner is left inside. Rinse the cloth many times. You don't want chemical residue when the customer uses the oven.

6

Scrub and dry the racks

Scrub the racks that were soaking. Rinse them. Dry them. Put them back in the oven. Close the door.

7

Clean the outside

Wipe the oven door, handle, and control panel with a damp cloth and all-purpose cleaner. Polish with a dry cloth.

🧊 Fridge Cleaning

A clean fridge is a healthy fridge. This job takes about 15 minutes.

1

Ask before removing food

Ask the customer if you can take the food out. If they are not home and the notes say to clean the fridge, gently remove items to a counter.

2

Remove shelves and drawers

Take out all removable shelves, drawers, and trays. Put them in the sink.

3

Wash shelves and drawers

Wash them with warm soapy water. Scrub off any sticky spots. Rinse and dry them.

4

Wipe inside the fridge

Use a mild cleaning solution (water + dish soap or a gentle all-purpose cleaner). Wipe all walls, the ceiling, and the floor of the fridge. Pay attention to corners and the rubber door seal.

5

Dry and reassemble

Dry all surfaces. Put shelves and drawers back. Put the food back. Wipe the outside of the fridge and the handle.

💡
Best practice: Work efficiently to minimize food temperature exposure. Keep the fridge door open only when actively working.

🛏️ Bed Linen Change

Fresh sheets make a home feel brand new. This add-on takes about 15 minutes per bed.

1

Remove old sheets

Take off the duvet cover, pillowcases, and fitted sheet. Put them in a pile — ask the customer where dirty laundry goes.

2

Check the mattress

If there are crumbs or hair on the mattress, quickly vacuum it. Straighten the mattress protector if there is one.

3

Put on the fitted sheet

Stretch the fitted sheet over all 4 corners tightly. No wrinkles. Pull it smooth.

4

Put on the duvet cover

Turn the duvet cover inside out. Grab the far corners. Clip the duvet corners and shake it down. Button or zip it closed. Lay it flat and neat.

5

Put on pillowcases

Put clean pillowcases on all pillows. Fluff the pillows. Place them neatly at the head of the bed.

📋
Note: The customer should have clean sheets ready for you. If they don't, ask where the clean sheets are. Never use dirty sheets.

🧖 Sauna Cleaning

Saunas are very common in Finland. They need special care. This add-on takes about 30 minutes.

1

Make sure the sauna is OFF and cool

Never clean a hot sauna. The heater (kiuas) must be completely off and cool before you start.

2

Remove loose items

Take out any towels, buckets, ladles, or seat covers. Move them outside the sauna.

3

Scrub the benches (lauteet)

Use a scrub brush and sauna-safe cleaner (or mild soap and water). Scrub the top and bottom of each bench. Scrub in the direction of the wood grain. Rinse with clean water.

4

Clean the walls

Wipe wooden walls with a damp cloth. Do not soak the wood — too much water can damage it. For stains, use a gentle sauna cleaner.

5

Scrub the floor

The floor is usually the dirtiest part. Scrub it with a stiff brush and cleaner. Rinse well with water. The drain should be clear — check it.

6

Wipe the heater area

Carefully wipe around the heater with a damp cloth. Do NOT touch the stones (kiuaskivet). Do NOT pour water on a cold heater.

7

Leave door open to dry

After cleaning, leave the sauna door open so air can flow and the wood dries properly.

🏢 Office Cleaning

Weekly or regular cleaning of small to medium offices. Usually done in evenings or weekends.

1

Empty all trash cans

Go to every desk and room. Empty every trash can. Put in new bags. This is always the first thing you do.

2

Wipe desks and surfaces

Use a damp microfiber cloth with all-purpose cleaner. Wipe all desks, tables, counters, and shelves. Be careful around computers — do not spray liquid near electronics.

3

Clean the kitchen/break room

Wash any dishes in the sink. Wipe counters, tables, and the microwave inside and out. Clean the coffee machine exterior. Wipe the fridge handle.

4

Clean the toilets

Scrub and disinfect toilets, sinks, and mirrors. Refill paper towels, toilet paper, and soap if supplies are there. Mop the floor.

5

Vacuum all floors

Vacuum the entire office including under desks and in meeting rooms. Vacuum rugs and mats.

6

Mop hard floors

After vacuuming, mop all hard floor areas. Use a damp mop — not soaking wet.

7

Final walk-through

Check that all lights are off (unless the customer says to leave them on). Make sure doors are locked if you have keys. Leave the office looking perfect.

🔒
Security: Do NOT touch private documents on desks. Do NOT open drawers. If you find a door locked, leave it. Report any security issues to your team leader.

🚽 Commercial Toilet Cleaning

Public and commercial toilets need extra disinfection. Hygiene is the #1 priority here.

1

Put on extra protection

Wear rubber gloves, and if available, a cleaning apron. Commercial toilets can be very dirty.

2

Spray disinfectant on all surfaces

Spray toilets, urinals, sinks, door handles, flush buttons, and taps with disinfectant. Let it sit for 5 minutes.

3

Scrub all toilets and urinals

Use a toilet brush inside. Use a scrub sponge on the outside. Clean the base of each toilet — this is where dirt hides.

4

Clean sinks and mirrors

Scrub sinks with bathroom cleaner. Polish faucets. Clean mirrors with glass cleaner. Wipe soap dispensers.

5

Refill supplies

Fill toilet paper holders, paper towel dispensers, and soap dispensers. If supplies are out, tell your team leader.

6

Mop the entire floor

Mop with hot water and disinfectant. Get under sinks and behind toilets. Make sure the drain is clear.

7

Check for smells

If it still smells bad, look for hidden sources (under toilet base, drain, behind pipes). Use air freshener if needed.

🍽️ Restaurant Cleaning

Restaurants must pass health inspections. Cleaning here must be very thorough. Grease is your biggest enemy.

1

Kitchen — Degrease all surfaces

Spray heavy-duty degreaser on counters, stovetops, prep tables, and hoods. Let it work. Scrub with a tough sponge. Wipe clean. Repeat if needed.

2

Clean grease filters

Remove the grease filters from the hood. Soak them in hot soapy water with degreaser. Scrub, rinse, dry, and put back.

3

Ovens and deep fryers

Clean according to the oven guide. For deep fryers: drain old oil (if asked), scrub inside with degreaser, rinse well.

4

Dining area

Wipe all tables and chairs. Clean the bar area. Wipe menus and condiment holders. Vacuum or sweep the floor. Then mop.

5

Bathrooms

Follow the commercial toilet guide. Restaurant bathrooms must be spotless — customers judge the restaurant by the bathroom.

6

Floors — Kitchen

Sweep the kitchen floor first. Then mop with hot water and degreaser. Kitchen floors get very greasy and slippery. Scrub stubborn spots.

🦺
Safety: Kitchen floors are slippery. Wear non-slip shoes. Be careful around hot equipment. Never use water near electrical panels.

🎪 Event After Cleaning

After a party or event, the space needs fast, thorough cleaning. Big team, fast work.

1

Collect all trash first

Walk through the entire space. Pick up all trash — cups, plates, napkins, bottles, decorations. Fill trash bags. This is the biggest job.

2

Remove decorations

Take down any leftover decorations, banners, or tape. Ask what to keep and what to throw away. Stack things neatly.

3

Wipe all tables and surfaces

Wipe down every table, counter, and bar area. Scrub sticky spots from spilled drinks. Use all-purpose cleaner.

4

Clean the kitchen/serving area

Wash any remaining dishes. Wipe counters. Clean the serving area. Take out food trash (it smells fast).

5

Clean bathrooms

Bathrooms after events are always messy. Deep clean and disinfect everything. Refill all supplies.

6

Vacuum and mop all floors

Vacuum the entire space. Look for confetti, food crumbs, and broken glass. Mop after vacuuming.

7

Move furniture back

If tables and chairs were moved for the event, put them back in their normal spots. Check with the customer or shift notes.

🪑 Conference Room Cleaning

Meeting rooms must look professional and smell fresh for the next meeting.

1

Clear the table

Remove any cups, water bottles, papers, and trash from the table. Throw away trash. Leave important-looking documents — do not throw those away.

2

Wipe the meeting table

Use all-purpose cleaner and a microfiber cloth. Wipe the entire table surface. Dry it so there are no streaks or wet spots.

3

Clean chairs

Wipe armrests and seat backs with a damp cloth. Check for stains. Vacuum fabric chairs if needed. Straighten all chairs around the table neatly.

4

Clean screens and whiteboards

Wipe TV screens gently with a dry microfiber cloth — never spray liquid on a screen. Erase whiteboards (unless notes say "do not erase").

5

Dust and vacuum

Dust window sills, shelves, and equipment. Vacuum the floor including under the table.

6

Set up for next meeting

If there are pens, notepads, or water bottles for meetings — put them back neatly. The room should look ready for use.

🏗️ Construction Cleaning

After building or renovation work. Very heavy cleaning — dust, paint, plaster, and debris everywhere. This takes 3× normal time.

🦺
Safety first: Wear gloves, mask (for dust), safety shoes, high-visibility safety vest, and hard hat that meet construction site work requirements. Watch for nails, screws, and sharp objects on the floor. Open all windows for fresh air.
1

Remove big debris and trash

Pick up large pieces — wood, packaging, plastic, leftover materials. Fill trash bags. Put them where the customer says.

2

Scrape paint and plaster spots

Use a plastic scraper to remove dried paint drops and plaster from floors, windows, and fixtures. Be gentle so you don't scratch surfaces.

3

Dust everything — twice

Construction dust is very fine and gets everywhere. Dust all surfaces from top to bottom. Then do it again. Wipe air vents and light fixtures.

4

Clean windows

Windows will have dust and possibly paint. Follow the window cleaning guide. You may need to scrape before washing.

5

Clean all fixtures

Wipe all door handles, light switches, outlet covers, radiators, and skirting boards. These collect a lot of fine dust.

6

Vacuum — then vacuum again

Vacuum all floors, corners, and edges. Use the nozzle attachment for tight spots. Then vacuum the whole space again.

7

Mop floors — multiple times

Mop all hard floors. Change the water often — construction dust makes water dirty fast. Mop at least 2–3 times until the water stays clean.

8

Final inspection

Run a white cloth along any surface. If it comes back dirty, clean again. Construction cleaning is not done until surfaces are truly clean.
